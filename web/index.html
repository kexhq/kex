Known limitation: anything that causes a process to actually yield — receive, receive timeout:, Task.await — currently misbehaves (duplicated output, lost state) due to an unresolved Asyncify/JS-interop issue in the wasm bindings. See docs/fiber-process-plan.md's "JS REPL bindings" section. Everything else — expressions, let/var, functions, records, pattern matching, and even spawn itself (just not waiting on a reply) — works correctly.
